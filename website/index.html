// what it is

Dictation that respects you

Most voice typing sends your microphone to the cloud. Quassel doesn't. Everything — recording, recognition, typing — happens locally. It just works, everywhere you type.

100% offline

Your audio never leaves the device. No cloud, no account, no telemetry — works with the network cable unplugged.

Even your terminal

Most dictation tools can't type into a terminal. Quassel can — and into any other text field too: editors, browsers, chat.

Open source (MIT)

Every line is auditable on GitHub. Free forever — no subscription, no upsell, nothing hidden.

Local Whisper engine

Powered by OpenAI's Whisper running on your hardware via whisper.cpp. Pick a model size to balance speed and accuracy.

Speaks your language

Whisper understands around 100 languages — Quassel transcribes them all, locally on your machine.

Your hotkey, your flow

Hold to talk, or double-tap for hands-free. A small floating pill shows status — and stays out of your way.

// how it works

Three steps, then forget it's there

01

Turn it on

02

Click into any field

An editor, a browser box, a chat window, even a terminal — anywhere your cursor blinks.

// questions

Frequently asked

Does my voice or audio ever leave my computer?
No. Recording and speech recognition both run locally through whisper.cpp. There is no cloud service, no account, and no telemetry — you can run Quassel with the network unplugged.
Is it really free?
Yes — Quassel is free and open source under the MIT license. No subscription, no trial, no paid tier. The full source is on GitHub.
Which languages does it understand?
It ships ready for English and German. Because it uses OpenAI's Whisper models, many more languages are available — you can pick the model that suits you.
Do I need an internet connection?
Only to download. The quick installer pulls the engine and a model that fit your hardware. After that Quassel runs fully offline — and the offline all-in-one packages need no internet at all, even to install.
Does it work on Wayland and X11?
Yes. Quassel runs on modern Linux desktops on both Wayland and X11, with systemd integration, as well as on Windows 10 and 11.
How accurate is it?
It uses the same Whisper models behind many commercial tools, just running locally. Larger models are more accurate; smaller ones are faster. You choose the trade-off, and you can switch any time.
